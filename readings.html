I have shared the links of some of the research groups and labs which I believe are conducting very good research.
The links shared below are solely based on my personal experience and suggestions from my friends.

Books

  • Linear Algebra Done Right by Sheldon Axler.

Video Sources

  • Advanced Matrix Theory and Linear Algebra by Prof. Vittal Rao(IISC).

  • Linear Algebra by Prof. Gilbert Strang(MIT).

Books

  • Principles of Modern Wireless Communication Systems by Aditya K. Jagannatham.

  • Introduction to Wireless Digital Communication: A Signal Processing Perspective by Robert W. Heath Jr..

MIMO and Massive MIMO :

  • Fundamentals of Massive MIMO by Thomas L. Marzetta, Erik G. Larsson, Hong Yang, and, Hien Quoc Ngo.

  • Massive MIMO Networks: Spectral, Energy, and Hardware Efficiency by \(\mathbf{Emil\;Bj\ddot{o}rnson}\), Jakob Hoydis and Luca Sanguinetti.

Millimeter Wave Communication :

Video Sources

  • Advanced 3G & 4G Wireless Mobile Communications by Prof. Aditya Jagganathan(IIT Kanpur).

Books

Video Sources

  • Quantum Mechanics and Quantum Computation by Umesh V. Vazirani (edX)

  • Quantum Information Science II, Part 1 - Quantum states, noise and error correction by Peter Shor and Isaac Chuang (edX).

\(^*\) : This subject require you to be good at both Linear algebra and Probability Theory.
\(^\dagger\) : This subject assumes proficiency in not only Linear algebra and Probability Theory but Convex Optimization also.
\(^\ddagger\) : Knowledge of Machine learning is important and Game theory is bonus but not necessary.
